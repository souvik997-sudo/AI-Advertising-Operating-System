## Version

# Introduction

The Prompt Templates module provides a standardized collection of reusable prompt templates within the AI Advertising Operating System (AAOS).

Its responsibility is to transform the Prompt Engineering Framework into practical, execution-ready templates that enable consistent AI interactions across different business requirements, marketing objectives, and automation workflows.

This module establishes a reusable prompt library that allows the AI to efficiently generate standardized prompts while maintaining quality, consistency, scalability, and alignment with the overall AAOS architecture.

---

## Scope of This Module

This module governs the organization, standardization, versioning, validation, maintenance, and reuse of prompt templates within the AI Advertising Operating System (AAOS).

It defines how prompt templates should be structured, classified, customized, and managed to ensure consistent execution across different AI models, business scenarios, marketing workflows, and automation systems.

This module does not define prompt engineering methodologies, business strategies, buyer psychology, copywriting principles, SEO methodologies, or visual advertising frameworks.

Those responsibilities remain within their respective AAOS modules.

---

## Relationship with Other Modules

The Prompt Templates module operates as the execution-ready template library built upon the Prompt Engineering Framework and other AAOS knowledge modules.

It consumes validated knowledge, prompt engineering standards, business frameworks, marketing strategies, and creative guidelines from other AAOS modules without duplicating their responsibilities.

The Prompt Templates module serves as the standardized repository of reusable prompt templates that enable consistent AI execution across multiple workflows, AI models, industries, and business scenarios.

# Purpose

This module defines how the AI should organize, select, customize, and execute reusable prompt templates for different business requirements, marketing objectives, and operational workflows.

The objective is to provide standardized prompt templates that improve execution efficiency, maintain output consistency, reduce prompt creation time, and support scalable AI-driven marketing operations.

The AI should intelligently select the most appropriate prompt template, adapt it to the specific business context, and generate high-quality outputs while maintaining consistency with the AAOS frameworks and standards.

---

# Template Philosophy

Effective prompt templates begin with understanding the business objective, the required task, and the expected outcome.

Every template should provide a standardized foundation that enables consistent, efficient, and high-quality AI execution while remaining flexible enough to adapt to different business scenarios.

The AI should prioritize reusability, clarity, contextual accuracy, and framework consistency over creating new prompts for repetitive tasks.

Well-designed prompt templates should reduce prompt engineering effort, improve execution reliability, support automation, and enable scalable AI-assisted marketing workflows across different industries, platforms, and AI models.

# Prompt Template Governance Principles

The following governance principles define the mandatory operational standards for all prompt templates within the AI Advertising Operating System (AAOS).

These principles ensure consistency, maintainability, scalability, interoperability, and enterprise-grade template management across all AI-powered workflows.

---

### TEMPLATE-001 Business-Driven Templates

Every prompt template must support a clearly defined business objective.

Templates should exist to solve repeatable business problems rather than isolated AI interactions.

---

### TEMPLATE-002 Standardized Template Structure

All prompt templates should follow a consistent structure to improve readability, maintainability, reuse, and execution quality.

---

### TEMPLATE-003 Reusability First

Templates should be designed for repeated use across multiple business scenarios, industries, AI models, and workflows.

---

### TEMPLATE-004 Placeholder Standardization

All variable information should be represented using standardized placeholders.

Templates should never contain hardcoded business-specific information.

---

### TEMPLATE-005 Framework Consistency

Every template should remain consistent with the applicable AAOS frameworks, governance standards, and architecture.

---

### TEMPLATE-006 Model-Agnostic Design

Templates should remain reusable across multiple AI models whenever possible.

Model-specific optimizations should extend the template without changing its primary purpose.

---

### TEMPLATE-007 Validation Before Execution

Every customized template must be validated before execution or deployment.

---

### TEMPLATE-008 Version-Controlled Templates

Every template should follow standardized versioning to support maintenance, updates, and backward compatibility.

---

### TEMPLATE-009 No Knowledge Duplication

Templates should consume validated knowledge from other AAOS modules rather than redefining business knowledge, copywriting, SEO, buyer psychology, or visual advertising principles.

---

### TEMPLATE-010 Continuous Template Improvement

Prompt templates should continuously evolve through validation results, execution feedback, workflow improvements, and AI capability enhancements while preserving architectural consistency.

# Placeholder Standards

Prompt templates should use standardized placeholders to represent variable information.

Placeholders improve template reusability, maintain consistency, and eliminate hardcoded business-specific content.

---

## Standard Placeholder Format

All placeholders should use the following syntax:

{{Placeholder_Name}}

Examples:

{{Business_Name}}

{{Industry}}

{{Product_Name}}

{{Service_Name}}

{{Target_Audience}}

{{Buyer_Persona}}

{{Brand_Voice}}

{{Offer}}

{{Platform}}

{{Campaign_Goal}}

{{Primary_Keyword}}

{{CTA}}

---

## Placeholder Rules

- Use descriptive names.
- Use PascalCase or Snake_Case consistently.
- Avoid ambiguous placeholder names.
- Never hardcode business-specific information.
- Every placeholder should have a defined purpose.
