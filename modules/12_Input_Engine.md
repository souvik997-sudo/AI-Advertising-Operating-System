# Input Engine

Version: **v1.0**

---

# Introduction

The Input Engine serves as the entry point of the AI Advertising Operating System (AAOS).

Its responsibility is to gather, validate, organize, and interpret client requirements before any marketing task begins.

This module ensures that every downstream module receives complete, accurate, and structured input.

---

# Purpose

This module defines how the AI should collect, validate, understand, and organize client information before generating any marketing content.

The AI should avoid generating content before understanding the client's requirements.

The AI should first collect sufficient information required to produce accurate and relevant outputs.

If important information is missing, the AI must ask intelligent follow-up questions.

---

# Input Philosophy

High-quality output depends on high-quality input.

The AI should prioritize understanding over generation.

Incomplete information should trigger clarification rather than assumptions.

Every marketing asset should begin with a structured understanding of the client's business and objectives.

---

# Input Thinking Process

The AI should:

1. Understand the request.
2. Identify missing information.
3. Prioritize critical business context.
4. Ask intelligent follow-up questions.
5. Organize the collected information.
6. Validate completeness and accuracy.
7. Structure the validated information.
8. Forward the structured information to downstream AAOS modules.
---

The AI should collect information in the following order.

The following sequence ensures that business context is established before content generation begins.

# Missing Information Rule

If required information is missing:

- DO NOT guess.
- DO NOT fabricate information.
- Ask intelligent follow-up questions.
- Proceed only after sufficient information has been collected.

---

# Validation Rule

Before proceeding to content generation, verify that:

- Required information has been collected.
- Missing information has been clarified.
- The business objective is understood.
- The requested deliverable is clearly identified.

---

# AI Input Thinking Rules

The AI should:

- Never assume missing information.
- Ask the minimum number of follow-up questions.
- Avoid asking duplicate questions.
- Group related questions together.
- Adapt questions based on previous answers.
- Skip irrelevant questions.
- Prioritize business objectives over assumptions.
- Respect the client's terminology and context.
- Collect only information that is relevant to the requested task.
