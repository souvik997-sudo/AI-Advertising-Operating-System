# Brand Research

Version: 1.0

---

# Overview

Brand Research is the process of collecting, organizing, and analyzing information about a business, its customers, competitors, and market environment before making branding or marketing decisions.

AAOS uses Brand Research as the foundation for Brand Analysis, Buyer Psychology, Marketing Strategy, and Content Creation.

---

# Objectives

The purpose of Brand Research is to:

* Understand the business
* Identify customer needs
* Analyze competitors
* Discover market opportunities
* Build stronger marketing strategies
* Reduce assumptions during AI-generated content creation

---

# Research Categories

## 1. Business Research

Collect information about:

* Business Name
* Industry
* Products & Services
* Mission
* Vision
* Brand Values
* Business Goals

---

## 2. Customer Research

Study:

* Target Audience
* Customer Demographics
* Pain Points
* Needs
* Buying Behaviour
* Emotional Triggers
* Customer Expectations

---

## 3. Competitor Research

Analyze:

* Direct Competitors
* Indirect Competitors
* Pricing
* USP
* Brand Positioning
* Marketing Strategy
* Strengths & Weaknesses

---

## 4. Market Research

Research:

* Industry Trends
* Market Size
* Customer Demand
* Emerging Technologies
* Seasonal Trends
* Growth Opportunities

---

## 5. Brand Research

Identify:

* Brand Identity
* Brand Personality
* Brand Voice
* Brand Positioning
* Reputation
* Customer Perception

---

# Recommended Data Sources

Collect research from trusted sources such as:

* Official Company Website
* Google Business Profile
* Customer Reviews
* Social Media Platforms
* Industry Reports
* Government Publications
* Market Research Reports
* Customer Interviews

---

# Research Checklist

Before starting Brand Analysis, verify:

* Business information collected
* Customer research completed
* Competitor research completed
* Market trends reviewed
* Brand identity understood
* Research findings documented

---

# AAOS Research Rule

Never generate marketing strategies using assumptions.

Every recommendation should be supported by reliable research whenever possible.

---

# Key Takeaway

Strong marketing begins with strong research.

Better research leads to better decisions, stronger branding, and more effective marketing.
